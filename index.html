Section 2 — Consistency & Effort
The Volume Gap Is Costing You
You have weeks with 3 quality sessions (March Wk3 is your benchmark: 8mi + 5mi + intervals) and weeks with just 1 or a complete blank. This variance is the single biggest limiter to your goals.
  • Strong weeks (Mar Wk3, Apr Wk1, May Wk4) prove your body can handle 3-session volume. That's the floor, not the ceiling.
  • Blank or 1-session weeks break metabolic momentum. Even a 2-mile easy run or 30-min incline walk maintains the signal.
  • For abs: you need the caloric deficit consistent week-over-week, not spiked then absent.
  • Target floor: 3 sessions/week, no week under 12 miles total. At current avg pace, 12 miles = ~1,400–1,600 kcal/week from running alone.
🔥 EPOC: The Hidden Calorie Burn
EPOC (Excess Post-Exercise Oxygen Consumption) is the elevated calorie burn that continues after your workout ends — your body repaying its "oxygen debt" by burning extra fuel to restore muscle glycogen, clear lactate, and return to homeostasis.

Your intervals generate the highest EPOC — up to +15% of session kcal for 24–48 hrs post-run. Fast runs under 7:30/mi add +8–10%. Easy runs add ~3–5%. All EPOC estimates are already included in each workout's calorie total on this page. The practical takeaway: a 500-kcal interval session can deliver 570–575 effective kcal toward your daily deficit.

Section 3 — Raise VO₂ Max
Tempo Runs: The VO₂ Multiplier
You're missing the threshold zone (comfortably hard, ~7:00–7:30/mi). This is where the most powerful VO₂ adaptations happen — raising your lactate threshold and cardiac stroke volume.
  • Protocol: 1 mi warmup → 3–4 mi @ 7:00–7:20/mi → 1 mi cooldown. Once per week.
  • At 185 lb and 7:10 pace, this burns ~580–620 kcal plus ~8% EPOC (+50 kcal) — comparable calorie density to your fast treadmill runs.
  • Expected VO₂ gain: +1 to +2 mL/kg/min per month of consistent tempo work. That's the fastest evidence-based path to your 58+ target.
  • Threshold work also increases fat oxidation at higher paces over time — your fat burn zone shifts upward, burning more fat even during fast runs.

Section 3 — Maximize EPOC + Calorie Burn
Pyramid Intervals: EPOC at Its Peak
Your current 6×¼mi intervals are excellent. The next level is pyramid intervals, which sustain elevated VO₂ longer and generate the highest EPOC response possible.
  • Protocol: 400m · 800m · 1200m · 800m · 400m with equal rest. All at 90–95% effort (~6:00–6:30/mi for 400s, slightly slower for longer reps.
  • Total ~3.4 miles of hard running. At your pace: ~520 kcal in-session + ~75–80 kcal EPOC = ~600 effective kcal.
  • This format spends more time at VO₂ max intensity than equal-distance quarter repeats — the key driver of VO₂ improvement.
  • Replace one ¼-mi interval session per week with pyramids. Keep one pure speed session for neuromuscular sharpness.

Section 3 — Abs & Body Composition
Fasted Incline + Strides Protocol
Two targeted add-ons that are low injury risk, high return for your specific goals of abs and overall toning.
  • Fasted incline walk (3–4x/week, 30–40 min, 12% grade): Before breakfast, your glycogen is depleted — body pulls almost entirely from fat stores. At 185 lb: ~260–330 kcal/session, ~62% from fat. Zero recovery cost, can be done on rest days.
  • Strides (2x/week, tacked onto easy runs): 6×20-sec accelerations to near-sprint pace at the end of an easy run. Stimulates fast-twitch fibers, improves running economy, adds ~+5% EPOC to the session. No extra time block required.
  • Hill repeats (1x/week): 8×30-sec hard uphill, walk down. Builds posterior chain (glutes, hamstrings) — the toning work that makes legs and core look defined. Generates high EPOC for short total time.
  • Combined weekly addition: ~900–1,100 kcal with minimal additional recovery demand.
